Getting Big Money Out of Politics: A Plan for Fair and Transparent Elections

The Facts: Democracy is for Sale

Money has overtaken American democracy, drowning out the voices of everyday citizens in favor of wealthy individuals, corporations, and special interest groups. The influence of mega-donors and dark money in our elections has created a system where those with the deepest pockets have the loudest voices, leaving the average voter with little say in the policies that shape our country.

The Scope of the Problem

  • Historic Election Spending: The 2024 election cycle was the most expensive in U.S. history, with a staggering $15.9 billion spent across all races.
  • The Candidate with the Most Money Wins: In 2018, 89% of House races and 84% of Senate races were won by the candidate with the most money.
  • Super PACs and Dark Money: Super PACs raised $4.29 billion in 2024. Dark money groups spent nearly $1 billion in federal elections between 2010 and 2020.
  • Billionaire Influence: In 2024, 150 billionaire families contributed nearly $1.9 billion to political campaigns.
  • Corporate Interests at Play: Companies like Wells Fargo and United Health Care pumped millions into Super PACs and lobbying efforts.
  • Corporate Lobbying is Out of Control: In 2024, Big Oil alone spent $243 million lobbying Congress.
  • Public Trust is at an All-Time Low: Voter confidence has plummeted as the system increasingly favors wealth over the public good.

The Solution: Publicly Funded Elections, Ending Corporate Influence, and Banning Insider Trading

Corporations and billionaires wouldn’t be spending billions of dollars on campaigns and lobbying if they weren’t seeing a return on their investment. Every dollar spent is meant to buy influence, shape policy, and ensure favorable legislation. This system has turned our democracy into a marketplace where access and decision-making are for sale to the highest bidder. Our solution aims to end this pay-to-play system and return power to the people.

We believe that elections should be decided by the people, not by the highest bidder. Our plan is simple: remove corporate influence, eliminate mega-donors, dismantle corporate lobbying power, and make all political campaigns publicly funded and fully transparent.

Our Goals:

  1. Enforce Strict Campaign Finance Laws
    • Prohibit members of Congress, the President, Vice President, Cabinet members, and senior officials from owning or trading individual stocks.
    • Require blind trusts to eliminate conflicts of interest.
    • Enforce strong penalties for violations.
  2. Ban Corporate and Special Interest Donations
    • End all corporate and union contributions, direct or indirect.
    • Eliminate Super PACs and independent expenditure groups.
  3. End Dark Money and Ensure Full Transparency
    • Require full disclosure of all donations.
    • Mandate real-time reporting of contributions and sources.
    • Impose strong penalties for concealment.
  4. Establish Publicly Funded Elections
    • Create a public election fund for qualified candidates.
    • Match small-dollar donations with public funds.
    • Guarantee equal access to funding for all eligible candidates.
  5. Limit Corporate Lobbying and End the Revolving Door
    • Cap lobbying expenditures per corporation.
    • Require disclosure of all lobbying activity and policy requests.
    • Enact a lifetime ban on lobbying by former elected officials.
    • Enforce strict penalties for unethical lobbying practices.
  6. Ban Stock Trading for Elected Officials and High-Level Government Officials
    • Public servants should not personally benefit from insider access to market-sensitive information.
  7. Amend the Constitution to End Corporate Influence in Politics
    • Overturn Citizens United v. FEC.
    • Clarify that corporations are not people and money is not speech.

A Democracy That Works for Everyone

This is not a partisan issue. It’s an American issue. The current system benefits only the ultra-wealthy while the rest of us are left without a voice in the decisions that affect our lives. By eliminating the influence of mega-donors, dismantling corporate lobbying power, and implementing publicly funded elections, we can restore democracy to the people.

It's time to take our government back. No more billionaires buying elections. No more dark money. No more corporate influence. Just free, fair, and transparent elections where every citizen’s voice matters equally.

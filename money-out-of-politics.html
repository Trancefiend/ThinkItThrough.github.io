Getting Big Money Out of Politics

A Plan for Fair and Transparent Elections

The Facts: Democracy is for Sale

Money has overtaken American democracy, drowning out the voices of everyday citizens in favor of wealthy individuals, corporations, and special interest groups. The influence of mega-donors and dark money in our elections has created a system where those with the deepest pockets have the loudest voices, leaving the average voter with little say in the policies that shape our country.

The Scope of the Problem

  • Historic Election Spending: The 2024 cycle cost $15.9 billion.
  • Money Wins Elections: In 2018, 89% of House and 84% of Senate winners outspent opponents.
  • Super PACs & Dark Money: $4.29 billion from Super PACs; nearly $1 billion dark money (2010–2020).
  • Billionaire Influence: 150 families gave $1.9 billion in 2024.
  • Corporate Interests: Companies like Wells Fargo & United Health Care fund Super PACs and lobbyists.
  • Lobbying Overload: Big Oil spent $243 million lobbying in 2024 alone.
  • Trust Eroded: Public confidence is at record lows.

The Solution: Publicly Funded Elections, Ending Corporate Influence & Banning Insider Trading

Corporations and billionaires wouldn’t spend billions without seeking returns. Every dollar buys access, shapes policy, and cements advantage. Our solution ends pay-to-play and returns power to the people.

Our Goals

  1. Enforce Strict Campaign Finance Laws
    • Ban stock trading by members of Congress, executives, and senior officials.
    • Require blind trusts for all public servants.
    • Impose severe penalties for violations.
  2. Ban Corporate & Special Interest Donations
    • End all corporate, union, and PAC contributions.
    • Abolish Super PACs and independent expenditure groups.
  3. Eliminate Dark Money & Guarantee Transparency
    • Mandate full, real‑time disclosure of all contributions.
    • Penalize concealment with hefty fines and criminal charges.
  4. Establish Publicly Funded Elections
    • Create a public election fund for qualified candidates.
    • Match small‐dollar donations to amplify grassroots voices.
    • Ensure equal funding access for all candidates.
  5. Limit Corporate Lobbying & End the Revolving Door
    • Cap lobbying spending per industry.
    • Require real‑time disclosure of policy requests.
    • Ban former officials from lobbying forever.
  6. Amend the Constitution to End Corporate Control
    • Overturn Citizens United v. FEC.
    • Reaffirm corporations are not people; money is not speech.

A Democracy That Works for Everyone

This isn’t a partisan fight—it’s an American fight. By eliminating mega‑donors, curbing corporate lobbyists, and funding elections publicly, we can restore our democracy to the people. No more billionaires buying elections. No more dark money. No more corporate control.
